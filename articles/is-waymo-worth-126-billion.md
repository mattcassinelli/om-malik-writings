# Is Waymo Worth $126 Billion?

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2026/02/12/is-waymo-worth-126-billion/)  
**Date:** February 12, 2026  
**Category:** Technology

---

Over the past three years, I have embraced three technologies almost completely. Generative AI, Apple's Vision Pro, and Waymo. I don't leave home without Waymo. I am probably one of their earliest adopters. And yet I am confounded by its valuation, especially given the challenges of growth.

Waymo "quadrupled" the number of trips in 2025—actually 3.3 times. About 4.5 million rides in 2024 and 15 million in 2025. Bloomberg reports that their annualized revenue run rate topped $350 million. That puts the valuation at 360 times revenue. $13 billion of that $16 billion round came from Alphabet. Currently the average fare for Waymo is roughly eighteen dollars per trip.

Unlike the voodoo economics of SaaS or AI, these are actual cars on actual roads. There are only so many rides a vehicle can give in a day. As of late 2025, Waymo had roughly 2,500 Jaguar I-PACEs on the road. Each vehicle currently does about twenty-five trips per day. To hit one million weekly rides with the current 2,500 vehicles, each car would need to complete fifty-seven trips per day. Physically impossible. Their fleet has to grow big and fast.

Waymo wants to expand to twenty cities in 2026, including London and Tokyo. Phoenix, the most mature market, took five years to reach its current scale.

Look, I get it. At present there is one company trying to do what Waymo is doing. Fully driverless, commercial, paid rides at multiple-city scale. I ride in one almost every day. The technology is real. The experience borders on magical. I feel far safer in a Waymo than with a human driver. That is why I wanted more from that Bloomberg piece. I want this company to exist. I wanted to understand whether the math works. Or is this a ride to nowhere?
