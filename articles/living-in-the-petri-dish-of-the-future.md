# Living in the Petri Dish of the Future

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2026/02/12/living-in-the-petri-dish-of-the-future/)  
**Date:** February 12, 2026  
**Category:** Technology

---

If you missed the brouhaha around a 5,000-word blog post about AI, let me bring you up to speed. HyperWriteAI CEO Matt Shumer's post "Something Big Is Happening" went viral this week. It had over 75 million views, though I can guarantee you very few people read the whole thing.

His thesis is that coding is the canary in the coal mine and other professions will follow. That's the meta point, and I don't disagree with it. What really kept me going was his Covid comparison. Shumer compared this moment in AI to February 2020, when a virus was spreading somewhere overseas and nobody was paying attention.

Months ago, a very smart friend of mine explained it differently. We had never lived the scientific process the way we did with the Covid crisis and the emergence of the vaccine. The trials, the debates, the reversals, the uncertainty—we watched science happen. It was messy, public, unfinished. We are doing the exact same thing with AI. We are living inside the experiment. The results aren't in. But that doesn't stop anyone from declaring them.

This whole drama isn't really about Shumer's essay. In the words of screenwriter William Goldman, "Nobody knows anything." We are living in a petri dish of the future. Some of us are hopeful. Some of us are terrified. Most of us are both. And into that vacuum of uncertainty there is a torrent of speculation dressed up as prophecy.

Every cycle produces its prophets and its skeptics. And every cycle, the reality turns out to be different from what either camp predicted. I embrace the uncertainty, the future, and let it all happen.

*February 12, 2026. San Francisco*
