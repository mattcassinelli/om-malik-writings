# Our Crazy Unhinged Now

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2026/02/04/our-crazy-unhinged-present/)  
**Date:** February 4, 2026  
**Category:** Technology

---

Waymo raised $16 billion this week. At a $126 billion valuation. I love Waymo. I use it multiple times a week. And yet, Waymo was the secondary story of the day.

Why? Because Elon Musk announced that SpaceX was acquiring xAI, creating a combined entity valued at $1.25 trillion. The deal could create a true full-stack company. Starlink for access. Million satellites for orbital compute. I love the vision. I buy the direction. I just don't buy the timeline.

The past decade has taught us this about Musk. The man loves his hyperboles. And that is Musk's real genius. Not the cars. Not the rockets. It is the storytelling. He understands that the real money comes from spinning the vision of the future. Reality is too real. What you need is future fantasy. The only other founder playing this game well right now is Sam Altman. He learned it from Elon.

Which brings us to the quietest bombshell of the week. Walmart crossed $1 trillion in market capitalization. The first traditional retailer ever to join the trillion-dollar club. For me, the Walmart story is really a meta-story. About Amazon. Amazon just laid off 16,000 people. That's on top of 14,000 in October 2025. When was the last time Amazon launched anything that can be called future forward? AWS in 2006, Kindle in 2007, Alexa in 2014.

So here we are. A $16 billion fundraise for robot cars became a secondary news story. A $1.25 trillion merger built on orbital compute dreams. A discount retailer from Arkansas joining the same valuation tier as Nvidia and Apple. We are living in amazing, bewildering, possibly unhinged times. Enjoy the show!

*February 4, 2026. San Francisco*
