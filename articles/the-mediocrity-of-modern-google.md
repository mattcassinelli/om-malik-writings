# The Mediocrity of Modern Google

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2025/03/29/the-mediocrity-of-modern-google/)  
**Date:** March 29, 2025  
**Category:** Technology

---

Whenever I think about Google, I recall Flaubert in Madame Bovary: "She wanted to die, but she also wanted to live in Paris." That is Google in a nutshell. The company that once represented the pinnacle of innovation has devolved into a symbol of corporate indifference.

I was an early Webpass customer. Post-acquisition? It's a different story. Google Fiber's Webpass feels stuck in time. The service interruptions have become far too frequent. But Webpass is just a symptom. Take Search. Using Google Search in 2025 feels like death by a thousand cuts—each query returning not answers, but an endless stream of AI-generated reviews and ads. This approach might please Wall Street, but it's pushing users straight into the arms of ChatGPT.

The company's recent Gemini announcement barely made a ripple. Meanwhile, OpenAI and Anthropic continue to dominate the AI narrative. What's particularly ironic is that today's Google has become exactly what its founders warned against in their 1998 paper: an advertising company whose business model fundamentally conflicts with serving users' needs.

Google is now deep in Phase Three, where internal politics and process management have overshadowed product innovation. Technology's self-obsolescence is a more effective regulator than any government agency. Google risks becoming the first of the mega-caps to fade into irrelevance as the internet evolves beyond the "10 blue links" paradigm.

I've voted with my wallet. I now use Kagi for search, and T-Mobile's 5G Home service for internet. In today's world, you simply can't rely on Google to do its job anymore.

*March 29, 2025. San Francisco*
