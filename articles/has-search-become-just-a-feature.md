# Has Search Become Just a Feature?

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2025/03/20/has-search-become-just-a-feature/)  
**Date:** March 20, 2025  
**Category:** Technology

---

On a typically gloomy San Francisco morning, while trying to sync my Philips Hue lights with Apple HomeKit, everything about the future of search snapped into focus. Instead of opening a browser and clicking through pages of results, I found myself simply asking ChatGPT about my HomeKit problems. The response wasn't a list of links but a synthesis of solutions. When I needed clarification, I didn't open a new tab—I just asked.

Claude's announcement of web search will likely reinforce behavior that's already becoming commonplace. By integrating web search capabilities, both Claude and OpenAI are transforming search from a destination into a feature—potentially sparking the next great upheaval of the internet.

This is just like how I felt when I experienced Google for the first time. After that first meeting with Google's co-founders, Yahoo, Lycos, and AltaVista suddenly felt antiquated. Search has evolved far beyond its 25-year legacy. In today's AI-driven landscape, search has become more conversational and integrated into everyday tasks.

I've witnessed several transformative moments: when Mosaic made the web browsable, when Google made it searchable, when Facebook made it social. This current shift feels similarly momentous. Even the browser is starting to feel like a relic. We're moving from a document-centric web to something more fluid, where information flows naturally through conversation.

The atomization of information is unfolding rapidly. AI doesn't just search; it synthesizes, contextualizes, and presents information in a user's preferred format. Consider Chegg, now suing Google because AI-generated answers are undermining its business model. Students aren't even going to Google anymore—they're turning directly to ChatGPT and Claude. With this evolution, we might finally be approaching what the internet was supposed to be all along: a tool for thought, not just a collection of pages.

*March 20, 2025. San Francisco*
