# How AI Goes to Work

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2026/02/06/how-ai-goes-to-work/)  
**Date:** February 6, 2026  
**Category:** Technology

---

Sometimes it takes a single piece in a jigsaw puzzle for the whole picture to come into shape. I was able to crystallize a lot of what I have been thinking about technologies colloquially known as "Artificial Intelligence." And it happened because of a 40-year-old piece of software that is used by tens of millions of people every day.

I've been writing about artificial intelligence for nearly a decade. In 2016, I argued in The New Yorker that we should think of it as augmented intelligence. Software that helps us deal with a world outpacing our capacity to process it. The argument never changed. The world just took a while to catch up.

This week, I downloaded Claude for Excel and started working on a spreadsheet tracking my spending over the past twelve months. After an hour of mucking about with Claude for Excel, it became clear that something was different. I wasn't spending my time crafting elaborate prompts. I was just working. The intelligence was just hovering to help me. Right there, inside the workflow, simply augmenting what I was doing.

This is the easy on-ramp to the big bad scary world of AI. What Claude did inside Excel, I have experienced as a photographer with Adobe Photoshop and Topaz AI. I've long been calling it augmented intelligence. Maybe it's time to redefine it as **"embedded intelligence."**

---

The history of technology offers great lessons. Before SaaS became dominant, software came as a bundle. A decade or so later came SaaS. That era has run its course. Fast-forward to today. With AI, we will soon live in a world where the workflows will be customized not just based on corporate but also on personal needs. What is a more likely scenario, at least in the near term, is what I experienced with Claude for Excel. AI showed up inside the tool I was already using. Without much friction.

Anthropic has been signing deals with data providers that people in finance actually use. This is verifiable information from sources that can be relied on for work. My friend Adam Bly's System does the same for the doctors at Mayo Clinic. DeepMind's spinoff Isomorphic Labs embeds intelligence into the drug discovery workflow. Embedded.

---

AI's progression is following the arc of any transformative technology. Every time technology prompts you to ask three questions. The first is existential. The second is epistemological. The third is practical—that is when it becomes invisible. Uber. Cloud computing. Same arc. AI, from my vantage point, is slowly entering that all important third phase.

Look no further than OpenClaw. You give it access to your applications and services; it watches your conversations, learns context, and handles tasks when asked. As an old timer this reminds me of Yahoo Pipes. IFTTT. Apple's Shortcuts remain the big missed opportunity. OpenClaw is on that curve. What OpenClaw shows is how AI will work in the background. Not a separate AI app. Intelligence woven into tools you already use.

For now, what I call embedded intelligence is a sensible on-ramp to the future. The hype may be about the frontier models. The disruption really is in the workflow.
