# The Greatest Invention is….

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2026/02/06/the-greatest-invention-is/)  
**Date:** February 6, 2026  
**Category:** Technology

---

Day One had a prompt: The most important invention in your lifetime is….

An invention is something that didn't exist before. It could be a device, a method, or a process that solves a problem in a way no one had quite thought of. My first thought was the microprocessor. Then the PC. Then the smartphone. The stent, as a cardiac survivor, may seem like the most important—I am alive because of it. But I narrowed it to something I had a living experience with, when I was coherent.

By that yardstick, the only invention I can really think of that weaves it all together is **the internet.**

I have seen it come together from its earliest days—the pipes, the software, the browser, the applications, the devices. Back in 1999, John Doerr commented: "Believe it or not, the Internet is actually underhyped." He called it "the largest legal creation of wealth in the history of the planet." Twenty-five years later, 10 out of the world's 12 trillion-dollar companies are internet or internet-related companies.

It is a great societal engine. Like all great inventions of the past—the wheel, the steam engine, the internal combustion engine—it has compressed time and distance. It has made us question every premise. What is a bookstore? What is a library? Who is a friend? What is reality, even? It is because of it that we are now looking at who we will be as a species.

So yes, the internet is indeed the greatest invention of my time. All other inventions, in one way or another, run through it.

*February 6, 2026. San Francisco.*
