# The Why of Substack

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2025/08/03/the-why-of-substack/)  
**Date:** August 3, 2025  
**Category:** Technology

---

Not for the first time, Substack is coming under criticism for hosting and promoting Nazi content. John Gruber is leading this crusade. They have controversies. Many express outrage. Some leave the platform. And Substack keeps growing. Why?

Why did they receive $100 million in new funding at a valuation of $1 billion? What have they built? Why do they have 5 million people paying for subscriptions? What is Substack really selling?

Eric Schmidt once pointed out that fiber optics and the wireless explosion would crush the business models of old media companies. All media platforms exist to sell "attention." What Substack has done is aggregate "reading attention" on its platform. Just as Facebook and Twitter wooed big media brands and celebrities, Substack wooed, paid, and promoted independent writers. These writers brought their audiences.

What Substack's critics miss is that the company has "network effects" working for it. Substack's network now drives 50% of all new subscriptions and 25% of new paid subscriptions. The paid subscriber base has exploded from 11,000 in July 2018 to over 5 million by March 2025. That's a nearly 500x increase in less than seven years.

Tyler Denk, CEO of Beehiiv, said: "I actually see Substack as building more of a social network now, like Twitter, Threads, Bluesky and LinkedIn." This conflation of platform with your own work is a hallmark of the media industry. The top 10 authors on Substack now collectively make $40 million per year. There are over 50 newsletters earning about a million dollars a year. That kind of exponential earning growth is hard to walk away from.

As for me, I am happy I have my own homestead on the Internet. I control every aspect of it.

*August 3, 2025. San Francisco*
