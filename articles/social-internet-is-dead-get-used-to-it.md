# Social Internet Is Dead. Get Over It.

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2023/10/15/social-internet-is-dead-get-used-to-it/)  
**Date:** October 15, 2023  
**Category:** Essays, Technology

---

The internet, as we have known it, has evolved from a quaint, quirky place to a social utopia, and then to an algorithmic reality. In this reality, the primary task of these platforms is not about idealism or even entertainment — it is about extracting as much revenue as possible from human vanity, avarice, and narcissism.

The Social Internet began as a place to forge "friendships" and engage in "social interactions." It performed its role until companies needed to generate profit. By then, we were all hooked on the likes, hearts, retweets, and followers. Back then, the internet was still seen as a utopian ideal — not a massive marketing machine. Sharing news and media links became an effective way for social platforms to keep users engaged. Hence, the social internet morphed into "social media."

Over the past few years, I've argued that there's nothing truly "social" about social media and that algorithms now primarily guide the flow of information. The algorithms are there to do two things — boost engagement and sell more ads.

Derek Powazek noted that Twitter was programmed to be an Argument Machine: "With enough people, and enough short thoughts, arguments are sure to occur. When they do, we'll add heat to them by making sure everyone can see the individual thoughts outside of the argument's participants."

Every tweet, every selfie is a chance to virtue signal, an opportunity to market yourself. We are addicted to the idea of an audience. The way social media is structured rewards extreme ideas, ideologies, and those on the fringe. Study after study shows that false news spreads faster than the truth. The problems have worsened. Where do we go from here? We must admit that the Social Web, as we knew it, is on its last legs.

*October 16, 2023. San Francisco.*
