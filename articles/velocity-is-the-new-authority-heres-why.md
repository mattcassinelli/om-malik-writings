# Velocity Is the New Authority. Here's Why

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2026/01/21/velocity-is-the-new-authority-heres-why/)  
**Date:** January 21, 2026  
**Category:** Essays, Technology

---

Why does everyone feel overwhelmed by information? The real answer has less to do with any single media entity and more with structural changes in the information ecosystem.

Authority used to be the organizing principle of information. You earned attention by being right, by being first in discovery, or by being big enough to be the default. That world is gone. **The new and current organizing principle of information is velocity.**

What matters now is how fast something moves through the network: how quickly it is clicked, shared, quoted, replied to, remixed, and replaced. In a system tuned for speed, authority is ornamental. The network rewards motion first and judgment later, if ever.

That's why we get all our information as memes. The meme has become the metastory. Algorithms on YouTube, Facebook, TikTok, Instagram, and Twitter do not optimize for truth or depth. They optimize for motion. A piece that moves fast is considered "good." A piece that hesitates disappears. People are not failing the platforms. People are behaving exactly as the platforms reward.

Take the launch of a new phone: when the embargo lifts, dozens of polished video reviews appear. The system rewards whoever speaks first, not whoever lives with it long enough to understand it. The "review" at launch outperforms the review written two months later by orders of magnitude. Once velocity is the prize, quality becomes risky. We get a culture optimized for first takes, not best takes.

The cost of all this isn't abstract. It's the review that took three months but no one will read. It's the investigation that required patience. All of it still exists, still gets made. It just doesn't travel. And in a system where only what travels matters, we've made expertise indistinguishable from noise.

*January 21, 2026. San Francisco*
