# The Land of Imperfect Data.

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2023/08/20/in-the-land-of-imperfect-data/)  
**Date:** August 20, 2023  
**Category:** Essays, Technology

---

The technology press is once again in an uproar over half-truths by Elon Musk, the owner of Twitter. He indicated that he's going to remove the "block" feature from Twitter and instead will force users to opt for "mute." The bigger question is: why would Twitter undertake such a move? I suspect the service's traffic and engagement are declining. Ending the "blocking" feature will help create a false impression of growth and usage. This isn't just a problem for Twitter; it's a widespread issue across our entire technology ecosystem.

Scottish poet Andrew Lang once said, "Politicians use statistics in the same way that a drunk uses lamp-posts—for support rather than illumination." The same argument can be made for technology companies. The half-truths in the data trotted out by technology companies show inflated growth, perceived engagement, and give a sense of overall well-being. You can manipulate the numbers to show what you want. Given the speed of our news cycle, no one will take the time to dig deeper.

I have doubts about the data and metrics shared by cloud companies—none of them are very transparent. Facebook buries the facts about fake accounts and duplicates at the end of its filings. By 2020, 16 percent of all Facebook accounts were false or duplicates—about 432 million. Twitter isn't immune either. Data researcher Travis Brown discovered that of the 153 million accounts following Musk, around 42 percent have zero followers. The median number of followers for all 153 million accounts following Musk is just one (1) single follower.

Musk's adeptness at using Twitter to influence sentiment around Tesla has enabled him to use the platform as his bully pulpit. Given the data analysis, there is little doubt that bots and spam are a big problem on Twitter—and only going to get bigger. The company has stopped spending money to fight that problem. By ending "block" and other such features, it is only going to use false data to paint a picture of growth and prosperity.

*August 20, 2023. San Francisco*
