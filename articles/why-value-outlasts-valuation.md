# Why Value Outlasts Valuation

**Source:** [On my Om](https://om.co/2025/11/06/why-value-outlasts-valuation/)  
**Date:** November 6, 2025  
**Category:** Technology

---

After a long innings in the technology industry and as an avid student of history, I have formed a simple personal thesis: **Value (and values) always trump valuations.**

A few days ago, the internet lit up with viral videos of Neo, a humanoid housekeeper by 1X Technologies. The review wasn't bad (but not good either). "Emphasis on attempt." As you read the review, you realize that there's nothing autonomous about this robot. It is teleoperated. Rodney Brooks, founder of iRobot, said: "The human form sort of promises it can do anything a human can. It's selling a promise that is amazing. There's a tendency to go for the flashy demo, but the flashy demo doesn't deal with the real environment."

Such attempts and stunts are to be expected when we are in a part of the technology cycle where valuations trump value. When value is perceived, customers are more than happy to change their behaviors. Facebook, Instagram, Uber, Amazon's S3. The rest of the world took its own time to catch up, and so did the valuation. When the technology cycle flips, valuation starts to trump value. It is easy to grow into your eventual valuation, but it is nearly impossible to do otherwise.

Remember the gig economy companies to do your laundry, park your car, or pick up household items for storage. The value was in on-demand mobility, and that is where the market settled: Uber and DoorDash. Vercel, Rippling, and Replit are doing well because they provide value to their customers.
